Report Submission Procedure

Groups are required to submit both an electronic copy and a hard copy of the following specific deliverables or reports:

  • Project Proposal-Draft A   (GROUP) (filename: "proposalDraftA")

  • Project Proposal-Draft B   (GROUP) (filename: "proposalDraftB")

  • Project Proposal-final draft (GROUP) (filename: "proposalFinal")

  • Progress Report  (INDIVIDUAL)  (filename: "progress")

  • Final Report (GROUP)               (filename: "finalReport")

The following are the general instructions for the submission process. Use the filenames listed above for the electronic submission. 

Hardcopy Submission

(For all deliverables except Project Proposal Draft B)
Submit your hard copy in the appropriate box for your section located in the hallway outside SFB560.
Click <here>  to find what section you've been/ assigned.

Project Proposal Draft B ONLY: Submit your hard copy to the Engineering Communication Centre, SFB670.
Before you drop in your document, book your appointment, sign up for a meeting time, and mark the meeting time code on the front page.

Electronic Copy Submission Procedure


1) Create your report according to the appropriate guideline available from the course website in 

Students --> Course Deliverables

3) Create an Acrobat (PDF*) version of your report and upload it to your ECF account.

* Refer to the website under 'Students' --> Other Resources' for details for converting Word documents to PDF

4) Depending on whether the report is an individual or group submission, there are two file naming conventions:

    Group reports:             <filename>-NNNNNNN.pdf 
    Individual reports:       <filename>-NNNNNNN-MMM.pdf 

  • <filename> is the type of report (follow the convention listed above) 
  • 'NNNNNNN' is your 7-digit project number (e.g. '2010555')
  • 'MMM' are your initials. Use 2 or 3 characters but make sure each member of the group uses different initials!

Example:  if you are submitting the group Proposal Proposal and your project number is 2010555, your file name would be "proposal-2010555.pdf". If you are submitting the individual Progress Report and your name is John Doe, your file name would be "progress-2010555-jd.pdf". The group part of the Progress Report should be submitted as "progress-2010555-group.pdf".

If you need to submit extra files, use an additional suffix after the project number and the appropriate file extension. 

Examples: "proposal-2010555-photo.jpg", "progress-2010555-jd-photo.jpg"


5) Submit your report from your ECF account.  Login into ECF and use the UNIX command 
'submitece496y <section_number> <file>' 

Example:      If your group is in Section 2, type 'submitece496y 2 proposal-2010555.pdf'.

6) You may check the status of your submissions by typing

submitece496y -l <section_number>

NOTE:  the first argument is a lowercase 'L' and not the digit one.

FINAL REMINDERS

  • Your report is late if you submit your hard- or soft-copy after the deadline (usually Tuesday at 3PM). Refer to Table 1 on the website under
       '    Students --> Schedules and Deadlines'
    for specific submission deadlines.
  • Make sure the Evaluation Form is attached to both the electronic copy and the hard copy and that the student section information is filled out.
  • Contact ask496@eecg  if you have any questions about this procedure. Lack of understanding of the correct submission procedure will not be accepted as an excuse for a late submission.
LATE REPORTS

Students submitting reports after the deadline are required to email an electronic copy directly to their Administrator, in addition to using the 'submit' utility. Students that are late should still use the 'submitece496' command but must submit their reports to Section 0 instead of  their own section in Step 6. Otherwise, the error message "Cannot make destination directory ..." will appear as all other sections will be closed by the deadline.

Late Penalty:

  • Reports submitted late but within 2 days after the due date receive an immediate 20% deduction.
  • Reports submitted more than 2 days will not be marked and receive a zero unless a course work petition is completed and accepted by the Administrator. Petition forms can be obtained from the Registrar’s Office.
  • Reports submitted late will likely be returned late, and there is no guarantee how soon they can be marked.
