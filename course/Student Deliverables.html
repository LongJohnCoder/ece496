Student Deliverables

Engineering design is the systematic, intelligent generation and evaluation of specifications for artefacts whose form and function achieve stated objectives and satisfy specified constraints.
     -Clive L. Dym & Patrick Little, Engineering Design

The design process in ECE496 is marked by 3 key milestones, the Design Review, the Progress Report, and the Design Fair. The course deliverables support these three milestones.

ECE496 Course Milestones and Deliverables Summary

Milestone Deliverables Description
 Design Review (START)   Project Proposal The main project planning document. Outlines the project, its motivation, the initial system design, and work plan. Students submit two earlier drafts to get comments from their Administrator (draft A) and from the Engineering Communication Centre (draft B), and defend their final version at the design review meeting .
  Design Review meeting Students defend their Project Proposal in a group meeting with their Administrator. Supervisors can attend.
 Progress Reports (MIDDLE)   December Review meeting A late-term meeting to report on progress and to correct deficiencies found at the Design Review meeting.
  Individual Progress Report One interim report from each student to document their progress to date. A supervisor evaluation of each student will also be given around the time this report is submitted.
  Group Oral Presentation A short oral presentation given by the team to the Administrator and to other students. The supervisor is invited to attend.
 Design Fair (END)   Final Report A final document of the group's work and final results.
  Design Fair A poster presentation and final demonstration of their completed project.

Notes:
  • performance graded on a group and individual basis by both supervisor and administrator
  • final grade is individually assigned and equally weighted between the supervisor and administrator

You must submit written work in both written and electronic form. The electronic form format is PDF. A second printed copy may be required if requested by your supervisor.

The purpose of submitting an electronic copy is as follows:
  • it allows the course administrators and supervisors to look back at your previous work just in case we feel that you have distorted the truth,
  • it allows us to archive all of the written deliverables and make them available to future students,
  • it allows supervisors to access past reports in case hardcopy or electronic versions of personally archived reports go missing or are accidentally deleted,
  • it allows those administrators that are more comfortable annotating documents electronically to do so.

The purpose of submitting the hardcopy is as follows:
  • for some people, a hardcopy is easier to read and easier to annotate. Also, working with a hardcopy is more time efficient for some people. There is no definitive proof for either of these two statements. Finally it comes down to a matter of preference. In summary, a majority of people still prefer working with a hardcopy so we are maintaining this standard.

Preparation:
Each student will be expected to have an engineering log book. This need not be elaborate or expensive (a spiral-bound soft covered notebook would do well). Some supervisors may insist on seeing what you have achieved on a weekly basis and your log book will be a good starting point for your weekly activity summary. In it you will keep notes, ideas, draft sketches, etc. Try to date each entry. These notes will be invaluable when you come to produce your deliverables.
